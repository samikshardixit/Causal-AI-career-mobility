Key Finding

The raw salary gap between AI-skilled and non-AI-skilled workers is ~32% in 2024. After matching on observable characteristics and using ChatGPT's launch as a natural experiment, the true causal effect is +8.8% — still economically significant, but far from the naive estimate.

Pre-trends Test

Coefficients for 2019, 2020, and 2021 are statistically indistinguishable from zero, confirming that treated and control workers were on parallel salary trajectories before the ChatGPT shock. The 2024 coefficient represents a +12.9% cumulative causal wage effect of AI skill acquisition.

Identification Strategy
Why This Is Causal, Not Just Correlational
01
Natural Experiment
ChatGPT's launch (Nov 2022) provides exogenous variation in AI skill adoption timing. No worker could have anticipated the exact shock, making it as-good-as-random for identification purposes.
02
Propensity Score Matching
Workers are matched on pre-treatment observables: industry, job family, seniority, AI exposure, and 2022 salary. This constructs a comparable counterfactual control group, reducing observable selection bias by ~50%.
03
Difference-in-Differences
Within the matched sample, DiD removes time-invariant unobservables (e.g. latent ability). Causal estimate β₃ = 0.0844 (SE=0.0029, p<0.001) with clustered standard errors at worker level.
